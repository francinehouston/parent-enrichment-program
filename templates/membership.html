Member Standards and Requirements

üìö

Student Athlete Attendance Requirements

To remain in good standing, each Student Athlete Participant is expected to maintain excellent attendance in their primary academic program and sports curriculum.

Minimum Standard

  • Goal of perfect attendance for classes and practices
  • Up to two (2) excused absences per academic term or season without a doctor's note may be allowed for illness, family emergency, or unavoidable conflicts
  • Additional absences must be documented with a school note, doctor's note, or approved communication with the program or coach

Failure to Meet Attendance Expectations

Failure to meet attendance expectations may result in:

  • Parent/Member check-in and support conversation
  • Probationary status for the member and/or student athlete
  • Temporary suspension from certain events, benefits, or incentives
üéâ

Event Participation Requirements

To remain an active P.E.P. member in good standing, each Parent/Guardian Member is expected to attend and/or support at least three (3) P.E.P. approved events per year, which may include:

  • Parent and athlete nights
  • Educational workshops and trainings
  • Community service events
  • Gala or recognition nights
  • Other official P.E.P. or partner events

Verified participants help maintain access to certain incentives, discounts, recognition opportunities, and advanced programming.

üìú

Mandatory Course Requirements

All parent/guardian members agree to complete the following core courses within a reasonable time frame established by the program.

P.C.O.R. Course

A Parent/Coach/Official/Relations-style course or equivalent focusing on leadership, communication, conflict resolution, and responsible sideline behavior.

CPR Certification

An Approved CPR or CPR/First Aid course recognized by the program, equipping members with basic life-saving skills and emergency readiness.

Additional Mandatory Courses

The program may designate additional required courses from time to time, such as:

  • Basic sports safety and injury awareness
  • Mental health awareness and referral basics
  • Program-specific compliance training

Important: Members are responsible for scheduling and completing these courses. Failure to complete required courses may place a member on probationary status or limit their access to certain benefits.

Sports Curriculum Enrollment

To align with the mission of supporting youth through athletics, each Student Athlete Participant connected to the program should be enrolled in a sports curriculum or organized sports activity, which may include:

  • School-sponsored sports teams
  • Community or club teams
  • Training or development programs partnered with P.E.P.

Code of Conduct

ü§ù

General Conduct

All members and participants shall:

  • Treat all youth, parents, staff, and partners with respect and dignity
  • Refrain from bullying, harassment, discrimination, or abusive language or behavior
  • Follow the instructions of coaches, officials, and program staff at events and trainings
  • Uphold sportsmanship, integrity, and fairness in all competitive and non-competitive settings
üõ°Ô∏è

Substance and Safety Policy

  • The use, possession, or distribution of illegal drugs or alcohol at P.E.P. events is strictly prohibited
  • Weapons and dangerous objects are prohibited at P.E.P. events unless explicitly authorized for security personnel by MMFP or a partner site
  • Any behavior that endangers the safety or well-being of youth or adults may result in immediate removal from an event and possible membership suspension
üì±

Social Media and Public Representation

Members should represent the program positively on social media and in public. Harassment, hate speech, or targeted attacks toward other members, staff, coaches, or partners may be grounds for disciplinary actions.

‚öñÔ∏è

Discipline, Probation and Appeals

Progressive Discipline

When possible, the program will use a progressive discipline model, which may include:

  • Verbal or written warning
  • Required meeting with program staff
  • Probationary period with written expectations
  • Temporary suspension from events or benefits
  • Termination of membership for serious or repeated violations

Immediate Suspension

In cases of serious misconduct, violence, or behavior that threatens the safety or well-being of others, the program may immediately suspend or remove a member or participant pending review.

Appeals

Members may submit a written appeal of a suspension or termination decision to the program's designated review committee or administrator within a reasonable time frame (for example, 10-15 business days). The decision of the review committee or administrator shall be final within the program.

Program Governance

  • The Parent Enrichment Program is administered by MannMade Fitness Productions LLC (MMFP)
  • MMFP may appoint a Program Director, Advisory Board, or Committee to provide oversight, review policies, and support decision-making
  • The Advisory Board (if established) may include parents, coaches, community leaders, and partner representatives

Membership in Good Standing

A member is considered in good standing when he or she:

  • Has completed required enrollment steps
  • Is current on any agreed membership obligations (if any fees or forms apply)
  • Has met or is actively working toward attendance and event participation requirements
  • Is actively pursuing completion of required courses (P.C.O.R., CPR, etc.)
  • Has no unresolved serious disciplinary actions or suspensions

Only members in good standing may be eligible for certain benefits, leadership roles, and/or advisory opportunities as determined by MMFP.

Member Rights

Members in good standing may:

  • Participate in program events, trainings, and incentives, subject to capacity
  • Provide feedback and suggestions to improve the program
  • Be considered for volunteer, mentor, or advisory opportunities when available
  • Access their own records of course completions and program participation

Member Responsibilities

All members are responsible for:

  • Maintaining updated contact information
  • Reviewing communication from the program in a timely manner
  • Modeling positive behavior for youth and other families
  • Respecting the decisions of coaches, officials, and program staff
  • Complying with all policies, rules, and bylaws

Amendments

These policies and bylaws may be updated or amended by MMFP as needed to reflect growth, legal requirements, and community feedback.

When material changes are made, MMFP will provide notice to members through reasonable communication channels (email, meetings, or posted updates).
